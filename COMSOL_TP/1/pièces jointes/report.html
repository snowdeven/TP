Laser Heating of a Silicon Wafer

Author COMSOL
Report date Nov 17, 2022 10:35:20 AM

Summary

A silicon wafer is heated up by a laser that moves radially in and out over time, while the wafer itself rotates on its stage. Modeling the incident heat flux from the laser as a spatially distributed heat source on the surface, the transient thermal response of the wafer is obtained. The peak, average, and minimum temperatures during the heating process are computed, as well as the temperature variations across the wafer.
